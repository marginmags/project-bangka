# Project Bangka — Personas

Three user profiles inform every design, UX, and product decision in Project Bangka. Every feature, onboarding flow, error state, and piece of copy should be validated against these personas.

---

## The Designer with Agency (Primary)

A designer ready to own their product vision end to end — not just design it, but ship it.

**Who they are:**
- Strong visual and UX instincts, fluent in Figma or similar tools
- Has a product vision and the intent to see it through — design, build, ship
- Navigating a new era where the line between designer and builder is dissolving
- Frustrated that the tools haven't caught up — design lives here, code lives there, and something is always lost in translation
- Capable of defining what they want visually and experientially

**What they need from Bangka:**
- Reliable translation of design vision into real, working product
- Ownership of design decisions, copy, hierarchy, and user experience
- Technical implementation handled for them, but always explainable in plain language

**What they are not:**
- Waiting for permission to ship
- Willing to hand control to an engineer and hope it comes back right
- Interested in learning to code — they want to build with the skills they already have

This is the designer in the next epoch of technology — where craft and agency are the same thing. They are the quality bar: **if something doesn't serve their workflow, it doesn't ship.**

---

## The Junior Designer (Secondary)

A designer early in their career who knows the process but is still developing the eye.

**Who they are:**
- Up to two years of professional experience
- Knows how to build wireframes, user flows, and follow a design methodology — they don't need to learn what design is
- Still developing taste and judgment — these come from reps, not coursework
- Driven by both curiosity and career pressure — genuinely exploring vibe coding and AI-assisted processes because they're curious, and because they sense the market shifting under them
- Underserved by tools that assume senior-level judgment or charge enterprise-level prices

**What they need from Bangka:**
- A way to ship real things so they can refine their craft through feedback, not just portfolio projects in Figma
- An on-ramp into AI-assisted building that doesn't require a senior engineer or enterprise budget
- Confidence in the relationship between their design decisions and the code that results

**The open-source argument:**
Project Bangka being open source is a direct statement to this person: **you shouldn't need a senior engineer or an enterprise budget to ship what you can already design.** Going from idea to something shareable — a live product, a real component, something a mentor can critique or a user can try — accelerates the feedback loop that develops the eye faster than any portfolio project sitting in Figma.

Vibe coding is where the industry is heading, but doing it well requires a mental model that designers are still developing. Project Bangka is a better on-ramp into the new way of working — not a shortcut around learning.

**How this persona evolved:** Originally framed as a "design student" — someone still learning fundamentals. Updated to "junior designer" because the target is someone who's *in* the industry, has process competence, but is still honing craft through real work. The shift matters: onboarding should never teach design fundamentals, but should acknowledge that judgment is still developing.

This persona is the accessibility check: **if they'd feel lost or intimidated, the UX needs rethinking.**

---

## The Creator as User

Project Bangka is being built by its primary persona — a designer living the problem this tool solves. The approach is personal by necessity: building for yourself is the fastest feedback loop, and the creator is actively thinking about peers with different expertise to shape the product as it grows.

This persona is the honesty check: every decision about complexity, language, onboarding, and workflow should be validated against the question **"would the person building this tool be able to use it?"** If the creator would find something confusing, intimidating, or unnecessarily technical — it needs to be rethought.

## How These Personas Work Together

| Persona | Role | Test |
|---|---|---|
| Designer with Agency | Quality bar | Does this serve their workflow? If not, it doesn't ship |
| Junior Designer | Accessibility check | Would they feel lost or intimidated? If so, rethink the UX |
| Creator as User | Honesty check | Can the person building this tool actually use it? |

When in doubt about any design, onboarding, or feature decision: does this serve the primary persona's agency? Does this alienate the secondary persona? Would the creator understand it?
